I have been solving sudoku puzzles since the age of 8 when my mother taught me how to do so. Throughout the years, I have made my own strategy tieing in what my mother taught me with my observations. My strategy implements minimum remaining value and forward checking to reduce and optimize variable constraint and selection. In order for my strategy to fully work, I had to implement a backtracking algorithm with a DFS stack to simulate the appropriate number placements.

My algorithm solved all 400 puzzles given to me in 20.8 seconds, which is an average of 0.05 seconds per sudoku puzzle. The quickest the algorithm solved a puzzle was 0.00347 seconds, while the longest took 0.6721 seconds.

It even solved the world's hardest sudoku.
