Relevant Coursework

I specialized in intelligent systems. Below are the graduate level courses I took in the specilization.

Artificial Intelligence

In this course, I learned about the basic techniques for building intelligent computer systems. I learned and implemented state-space problem representations, problem reduction and and-or graphs, game playing and heuristic search, backtracking, predicate calculus, and resulition theorem proving, AI systems and languages for knowledge representation, and machine learning and concept formation.

Natural Language Processing

In this course, I learned how to create systems that can analyze, understand and produce language. I also learned and applied machine learning methods for NLP as well as core NLP, such as language modeling, part of speech tagging (POS) and parsing. I learned applications such as information extraction, machine translation, text generation and automatic summarization. I learned about statistical and machine learning based approaches to language processing, and about the use of linguistic concepts that play a role. I studied and implemented machine learning methods currently used in NLP, including supervised machine learning, hidden markov models, and neural networks.

Computer Vision

In this course, I was introduced to fundamental and advanced topics in computer vision such as image formation and optics, image sensing, binary images, image processing and filtering, edge extraction and boundary detection, region growing and segmentation, pattern classification methods, brightness and reference, shape from shading and photometric stereo, texture, binocular stereo, optical flow and motion, 2D and 3D object representation, object recognition, vision systems and applications.

Visual Interfaces to Computers

In this course, I explored existing research and commercial systems that allow computers to accept and manipulate visual input, usually from real time cameras, in order to direct further computer processing. I learned and implemented how to recognize and interpret a human's hand, head, and body gestures for control information, how to retrieve from, or summarize the content of, a database of images or videos, how to generate natural language descriptions of visual and spatial relationships, how to monitor large areas for types of unusual activity, how to analyze fingerprint, iris, and facial patterns to obtain security data, how to determine spatial locations visually and steer cars automatically, and how to estimate emotional content in images and video. I also explored foundations in human psychophysics, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence.

Machine Learning

In this course, I learned and implemented generative and discriminative machine learning methods such as least squares methods, support vector machines, kernel methods, neural networks, Gaussian distributions, linear classification, linear regression, maximum likelihood, exponential family distributions, Bayesian networks, Bayesian inference, mixture models, the EM algorithm, graphical models and hidden Markov models.
